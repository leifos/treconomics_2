Experiment Instructions

First of all, thank you for agreeing to participate in this experiment about online searching!
During this experiment we would like you to perform four different search tasks (and a practice task) using the same search system.

Your Role

  • For this experiment, imagine that you heard about an interesting topic in the news, and you want to find out more information about this topic in order to write a blog post about it. In order to accomplish this you will need to search for related information to find relevant documents that will help you write your blog.

The Search System

  • The search system that you will use connects to a database of about 1 million newspaper articles from the New York Times, The Associated Press and Xinhua news agencies. The articles within the database range from 1999 to 2000. The search system you'll be using looks similar to a web search engine, such as Google.

The Search Tasks

  • For each search task, you'll be given a short description of a topic and a clue at what kind of information would be relevant to this topic. This can be viewed by the Show Task button at the top right-hand corner of the screen.

  • You will pose queries and look at results, just like you would in Google. When viewing documents, you can select the Mark as Relevant button when you find a document relevant to the topic. A relevant document would be one which you think could hypothetically help you to write your blog. There are over 100 relevant documents in the database for each topic.

Goal and Incentive

  • As previously mentioned, your goal is to find a range of different examples so you can provide a balanced view of the topic.
  • The experiment should take approximately 1 hour

Experiment Outline

Before you start searching, we will ask you a few questions about you.

We will then give you a few minutes to get comfortable with the screen by undertaking a practice task.

You'll then move onto the four individual main search tasks. Before each task, you will complete a pre-task questionnaire about your knowledge of the search topic. After you complete the task, you will complete a post-task questionnaire about the search experience.

At the end of the experiment, there will be one final questionnaire about your overall experience.

Confirmation of Participation and Commitment

If you have any questions, please ask the researcher now. Please remember that the data you provide will be anonymous, and will be used only for the purposes of this study.

If you are happy to undertake the experiment described above and committed to doing your best on the search tasks, then please click the Next button below.
