Experiment Instructions

First of all, thank you for agreeing to participate in this experiment about searching for news.
During this experiment we would like you to perform four different search tasks (and a practice task) using our news search system.

Your Role

Imagine that you are taking a journalist class and need to write a number of short posts/articles on various topics about the state of the world at the turn of the century.

To provide the evidence for your articles, you need to find several different examples for each topic to demonstrate your breadth of understanding about the topic, i.e. you want to be able to discuss a number of different examples for each topic.

The Search System

The search system that you will use connects to a database of about 1 million newspaper articles from the New York Times, The Associated Press and Xinhua news agencies. The articles within the database range from 1996 to 2000. The search system you'll be using looks similar to a web search engine, such as Google.

The Search Tasks

For each search task, you'll be given a short description of a topic and a clue at what kind of information would be relevant to this topic. This can be viewed by the Show Task button at the top right-hand corner of the screen.

You will be able to enter queries and look at results, just like you would in a normal search engine. When viewing documents, you can select the Mark as Relevant button to save document with good examples.

A relevant document should meet the specified criteria and help you to provide evidence for your article. After searching, you will be asked to list as many of the examples that you have found as possible.

Goal

Your goal is to find and save several relevant and different examples for each topic

Experiment Outline

The experiment should take approximately one hour in total i.e. about 10-15 minutes per task.

Before you start searching, we will ask you a few questions about you.

We will then give you a few minutes to get comfortable with the system by undertaking a practice task.

You'll then move onto the four individual main search tasks. Before each task, you will complete a pre-task questionnaire about your knowledge of the search topic. After you complete the task, you will complete a post-task questionnaire about the search experience.

At the end of the experiment, there will be one final questionnaire about your overall experience.

Confirmation of Participation and Commitment

If you have any questions, please ask the researcher now. Please remember that the data you provide will be anonymous, and will be used only for the purposes of this study.

If you are happy to undertake the experiment described above and committed to doing your best on the search tasks, then please click the Consent and Confirm button below.
